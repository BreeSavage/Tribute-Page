N.K Jemisin

First African American to win the Hugo Award for Best Novel

    Accomplishments

  • Locus Award, Best First Novel (2011)
  • Hugo Award, Best Novel (2016)
  • Hugo Award, Best Novel (2017)
  • Hugo Award, Best Novel (2018)
  • Nebula Award, Best Novel (2018)
  • Locus Award, Best Novel (2018)

Early Life and Career

Jemisin was born Nora K. Jemisin in Iowa City, Iowa in 1972. She earned a Bachelor of Science in Psychology before going on to earn her Master of Education. She worked primarily as a counseling psychologist while writing science fiction short stories on the side. In 2016 Jemisin began writing a science fiction book review coloumn for the New York Times entitled, "Otherworldly". In 2016 she also started a successful Patreon campaign to fundraise enough money to allow her to quit her "day job" as a psychologist and focus full time on writing. Jemisin is most well known for her Broken Earth series which earned her the Hugo Award for Best Novel three years in a row! She is the only author ever to have achieved this feat.
