N.K Jemisin

First African American to win the Hugo Award for Best Novel

About

Jemisin is a self-described hiker and biker and is a "political, feminist, anti- racist blogger". She is advocate for equality and is passionate in exploring cultural conflict and oppression. Her works discuss important topics such as gentrification, the patriarchy and human interaction with the environment. She currently resides in Brooklyn, New York where she writes at a desk overlooking her garden. Jemisin recently released the first novel in a new trilogy. It is called The City We Became.

Early Life and Career

Jemisin was born Nora K. Jemisin in Iowa City, Iowa in 1972. She earned a Bachelor of Science in Psychology before going on to earn her Master of Education. She worked primarily as a counseling psychologist while writing science fiction short stories on the side. In 2016 Jemisin began writing a science fiction book review coloumn for the New York Times entitled, "Otherworldly". In 2016 she also started a Patreon campaign to fundraise enough money to allow her to quit her "day job" as a psychologist and focus full time on writing. Her success with her Patreon campaign paid off when in that same year Jemisin became the first African American to ever win the Hugo Award for best Novel. Jemisin is most well known for her Broken Earth series which earned her the Hugo Award for Best Novel three years in a row. She is the only author ever to have achieved this feat.
